abstract: 'A synthesis of the Phaeogenini occurring in the Afrotropical region is provided. Three species are newly described: <i>Centeterichneumon nambi</i> Dal Pos, Diller & Di Giovanni sp. nov. from Uganda, <i>Chauvinia ganota,/i> Claridge sp. nov. from Kenya, and <i>Kibalus nonnaritae</i> Dal Pos & Di Giovanni sp. nov. from Uganda. <i>Heterischnus mfongosi</i> Rousse & van Noort, 2013 is newly recorded for Kenya and Tanzania and the male of the species is diagnosed for the first time. Also, the female of Arearia oxymoron Rousse & van Noort, 2013 is diagnosed for the first time from one of the paratype localities. <i>Lusius tenuissimus</i> (Heinrich, 1938) and <i>Chauvinia nyanga</i> Rousse & van Noort, 2013 are recorded for the first time for Uganda and Kenya, respectively. In addition, new localities are given for <i>Chauvinia nitida</i> (Heinrich, 1938), <i>Heterischnus olsoufieffi</i> (Heinrich, 1938) and <i>Hoplophaeogenes curticornis</i> Heinrich, 1938. A new combination, <i>Nesostenodontus mkomazi</i> (Rousse & van Noort, 2013) comb. nov., is proposed to accommodate <i>Heterischnus mkomazi</i>. An updated key to the Afrotropical genera of Phaeogenini and keys to the Afrotropical species of the genera <i>Arearia</i> Seyrig, <i>Centeterichneumon</i> Heinrich, <i>Chauvinia</i> Heinrich, <i>Heterischnus</i> Heinrich, <i>Hoplophaeogenes</i> Heinrich, <i>Kibalus</i> Rousse, van Noort & Diller, and <i>Lusius</i> Tosquinet are provided. Updated online Lucid keys to genera and species are available from http://www.waspweb.org.'
